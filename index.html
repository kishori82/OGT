Statement of the Trustees

Ocean Gate Tower Condominium is a unique property because it is a condominium association of 111 residential units and 3 commercial units located in one high-rise building. When one elects to purchase a condominium, there is a shared ownership and shared responsibility. The degree to which residents respect each other's rights ultimately shapes the quality of life at Ocean Gate Tower Condominium. Rules, regulations and restrictions are necessary to preserve your right to the enjoyment of your community. In that regard, it is incumbent upon the Ocean Gate Tower Condominium Board of Trustees, acting through the Management Company/Agent(s), to invoke all measures necessary to enforce the rules, regulations, and restrictions. The cooperation of all Unit Owners and Residents is expected.

A five-member Board of Trustees, whose members are elected to a three- year term by the Unit owners at the annual meeting in May, heads the organizational structure of Ocean Gate.

The Board of Trustees mandatory meets on the day of the Annual Budget Meeting of the Unit Owners that is usually held on the first or second Wednesday of December. The announcement has to be posted in a visible place at least 48 hrs in advance. The Board may elect to call other meetings during the year as often as they deem necessary to ensure the normal course of business of the Association for the benefit of all members of the Association. Typically, there is one more unit owners' meeting for the election of Trustee's in May of each year.

Unit Owners are welcome to attend and observe the open sessions of the Board of trustees' meetings, if an open session is announced. Meetings are usually held in the second floor Community Room. The Board may invite Unit owners and others to attend meetings as guests in an advisory capacity.

The Declaration of Trust empowers the Board of Trustees with the authority to manage and maintain the condominium. Company/Agent(s), hired by the Board, are responsible for the day-to-day operations of the Ocean Gate Tower Condominium Trust (OGT), carrying the instructions from the Board, and in general, acting to serve the best interest of all Unit Owners as a community. The Management Company/Agent's responsibilities include such tasks as: the collection of monthly condominium fees and assessments; the processing of bills; managing the selection process of contractors, overseeing their work and inspecting their services for Common Areas; enforcement of the condominium rules and regulations; procurement of insurance coverage; and the supervision of employees of OGT to ensure efficient maintenance and repairs of the Common Areas.

We are delighted that you have chosen to live in one of the finest communities in the greater Boston area. It takes coordination and communication among the entire community and its valuable volunteers to maintain Ocean Gate Tower Condominium. This Handbook is intended to be a handy reference for rules, regulations and guidelines that help keep OGT a pleasant place to live. It was created in 2008 and incorporated 2 important Resolutions of the Board of 2008: Unit Maintenance Resolution and Insurance Resolutions, as well as new Rules on Community Room Reservation and Use, and Rental/Sales Rules. The Handbook is constantly updated and new Rules and Regulations are added to it following the ever changing life in our community. Updates are usually placed at the beginning of the Handbook.

The Handbook provides answers to the most frequently asked questions and concerns with respect to condominium life at Ocean Gate. This Handbook summarizes what is found in more complete and explicit form in the Master Deed and Condominium Trust Documents you received at the time of purchase. These documents are the final authority on rules and regulations.

Ocean Gate Tower Condominium Rules and Regulations

  1. Common Areas and Facilities
  1. Unit Owners shall not cause, nor shall they suffer, obstruction of the common areas and facilities except as the Trustees may, in specific instances, permit. If such obstruction has been erected it should be willingly and immediately removed by the guilty party at the request of another unit owner, trustee or management
  2. No articles, including without limitation, doormats, umbrellas or boots, shall be left by Unit Owners in the corridors or other common areas of the Condominium, nor may any decoration be attached to corridor doors or walls, or mirrors. Unit owners may place their names only in such a place outside of the Unit as may be provided for them by the Trustees.
  3. No baby carriages, bicycles, playpens, wagons, toys, benches, chairs or other articles of personal property shall be left unattended in the common areas of the building, cargo area, parking areas inside or outside the garages, sidewalks, lawns or elsewhere in the common area except as those areas specifically designated by the Trustees or should they be used for playing or creating any type of disturbance.

  1. Storage Areas

No items shall be stored in common areas. All personal property placed in any portion of the common area shall be at the sole risk of the Unit owners, and the Association shall not be liable for loss, destruction, theft or damage to such property. No locks on lockers in the women and men's locker rooms in the pool are allowed. No personal items should be left behind in the lockers. The use of the lockers is for the duration of the time actually spent by the OGT residents at the pool area.

Common Storage Room

A common storage room is located on the second floor and intended for small items only. The storage room has shelving with designated space for each unit. The area for each unit is 24" x32" approximately 5 1/3 Square Feet. OGT Staff presence is required during storage or removal of items.

Storage Room Guidelines (displayed in the storage room from 2003):

  1. All items must fit within the allotted space for each unit.
  2. Items must be labeled with Name and Unit #.
  3. Items must not be stored in walkways, along the walls or door, or block any passage within the storage room.
  4. Tires, machinery such as air conditioners, exercise equipment and bulky sporting equipment, bulky toys and decorations are not allowed.
  5. All stored items must comply with fire codes.
  6. No hazardous materials including but not limited to: flammables, poisonous substances, acids, order caustic materials, explosives, etc
  7. Access is available only during hours when OGT personnel is present
  8. Staff to record activity on daily log of whom they let place or remove items. All items stored in this room are at the sole risk of the owner of items stored. Every effort will be made by staff to keep this room secure at all times.

  1. Pets

No pets shall be raised, bred or kept in a Unit or in the common areas.

  1. Windows, Balconies, and Patios

  1. Each Unit Owner shall keep the balcony or patio, if any, of which the Unit has the exclusive right and easement of use, clean and neat at all times, at the Unit Owner's sole cost and expense. The floor of the balcony can be painted or tiled only with prior approval of the Board of Trustees to prevent deterioration of concrete.
  2. No balcony or patio enclosures may be erected without the written permission of the Trustees upon such terms and conditions, if any, as they, in their reasonable discretion, shall determine.
  3. No awnings, antennae, aerials, signs, umbrellas, banners, flags (other than USA flag) or other alterations or projections shall be attached to the outside walls of the buildings, or hung or displayed from any windows, balconies or patios, including without limitation "For Sale" or "For Rent" signs.
  4. Satellite Dishes are allowed per FCC ruling, however, limited by the Association's Installation Policy. Reception of Satellite Dishes is difficult and some units cannot receive any signals; check for signals before any installation begins. Owner, Tenant and Installer must comply with the following: Satellite Dishes are allowed but must be limited to one per unit, 18" or less in diameter and installed by a licensed and insured contractor. The Association requires installation of satellite dishes to be only installed from the balcony rail using a clamp that is removable. The satellite dish cannot protrude over the edge of the balcony. No attachment to the exterior walls is permitted. Holes for wires may only be made through the unit owner's doorframe. Wiring shall be secured and not seen from outside the building. Any holes drilled through the exterior of the building are the unit owner' responsibility and must be sealed to prevent leaks. It is strictly forbidden to use any area other than the limited common use area of balconies, patios, or decks. No installation is allowed on the roofs or exterior walls.
  5. Neither balconies nor patios shall be used for barbequing or for the storage of furniture or other articles.
  6. No laundry, rugs, drapes, mops or other items shall be shaken from or hung outside any window, patio or balcony, nor shall any dirt or other substances including snow, dry leaves etc. be swept or thrown from a balcony or patio or window.
  7. Unit Owners shall not be permitted to install any additional heating or air conditioning equipment through or from any walls, windows, balconies or patios. Only the evacuation of hot air through an exhaust hose of a portable indoor air conditioning unit through the natural window opening maybe allowed, if it does not cause outside the window drainage.
  8. The exterior surfaces of all window shades, window curtains and other window treatment shall be white in color.

In the event of such abuse or misuse, the Trustees may charge the responsible Unit Owner for any damage so caused. No electrical outlets located in the common areas shall be used by individual unit owners unless it is used for the benefit of the community. No sweepings, other solid debris or hazardous materials shall be dumped, washed or flushed into the building's plumbing system.

In the event that any damage shall be done to the elevator during such use, the unit owner shall be liable for restitution. The Trustees shall have the power to assess monetary fines for the violations of these Rules and Regulations, which fines shall constitute a lien against the Unit Owners assessed, and the Trustees shall also have the power to enjoin, abate or remedy the breach of any of these Rules and Regulations by appropriate legal proceedings, all as further provided in section 5.8 of the Ocean Gate Tower Condominium Trust Declaration.

The Board of Trustees reserves the right to impose a higher fine and/or restitution in especially offensive cases such as life/safety or property damages.

Any fines that remain unpaid after fifteen days shall be assessed to the Unit owner as lien on his or her Unit.
